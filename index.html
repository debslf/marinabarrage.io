MARINA BARRAGE

Singapore's largest and most urbanised water catchment
Leisurely strolls, family days and picnics with a view. Check it out for yourself!


The Marina Barrage is a dam in Singapore built at the confluence of five rivers, across the Marina Channel between Marina East and Marina South. First conceptualised in 1987 but the late Lee Kuan Yew, the Barrage started construction on 22nd March 2005, and was officially opened on 31st October 2008 as Singapore's fifteenth reservoir. It provides water storage, flood control and recreation. It won a Superior Achievement Award from the American Academy of Environmental Engineers in 2009.

Picnics

Once you've had enough fun in the sun, do head up to the Green Roof .

The Green Roof at Marina Barrage is a popular location to spend some time with your family and friends. The windy nature of the location allows for large-scale gatherings (pre-Covid times) and for kids to run around, having fun and flying kites.

On top of gatherings, large-scale events such as parties, wedding receptions, corporate events, award presentations and community functions are also subject to approval.

For enquiries on rates or event booking approval, please email to PUB_MB_Events@pub.gov.sg




Opening hours for the Green Roof are as follows:

  • Mondays to Thursdays: 8am-9pm (8pm last entry)

  • Fridays to Sundays and Public Holidays: 8am-10pm (9pm last entry)


    • In view of Covid-19, please remember to maintain a safe distance away from other visitors and ensure restrictions are followed strictly.
    The Green Roof may be temporarily closed when capacity limit is reached.

    Singapore Sustainable Gallery

    Housed at Marina Barrage is the Sustainable Singapore Gallery . Sustainability has always been integral to Singapore's development since independence, and we have been tackling challenges to ensure that everyone continues to enjoy a clean and green environment.

    Opening Hours: 9am-6pm Daily (closed on Tuesdays)
